Letter from Babacar

We all encompass so many causes to embrace and identities to claim. There are times we find ourselves torn in between identities that are privileged and underprivileged, a victim and a perpetrator. Am I a man in this patriarchal society, a griot in Senegal, or a black man in this global society? I tell my students on Bridge Year, there are at least four answers to any question. And there are many identities in all of us humans. I am a father, a brother, a friend, a husband, a host, a guest, a threat, an ally. I embrace this complexity that forms me, a human being. . .

Our Mission

Nu Jange Ci: Intercultural Learning Mediation is an experiential-learning program for Senegalese youth in Yoff, Dakar run by Babacar Mbaye, a teacher for over twenty years at three different institutions, across the globe and across Senegal. The program is intended to empower students to “come together across identities, borders, prejudices, emotions, and histories to exercise compassion responsibly.”
