Babacar Mbaye

My life has always been about education. It has started with the joy of learning, understanding, and being able to do things. Then it was all about service; reading prescriptions, writing letters, and mentoring friends and siblings. Now, I am an educator. It means I am a teacher, a mentor, a friend. an advocate and an activist. It's been more than twenty years now.

I am also a learner indebted to dozens of teachers all around the world. There have been unexpected lessons from people of various walks of life in unexpected places. I have been humbled thousands of times by young or old, smart or wise, rich or poor, powerful or otherwise but always beautiful humans.

I was made by men and women whose struggles and successes form, inform, and transform me. My goal is to contribute to the making of the future of this planet. I am taking the courage and responsibility with all fairness and humility.

Maguette Fall

My name is Maguette Fall, and I am a preschool educator involved with the broader educational community. I want to be apart of Nu Jange Ci to better develop my skills and strengthen my teaching curriculum. Thanks to this program I can bring my support to the children through educational outings and discoveries of our environment during the work sessions. I believe NJC is a framework that will better help our children learn at school and understand their general culture. During this period of Covid-19, taking action for our children is a good thing and I look forward to working with the scholars.

Keba Mane

Keba Mane is a language, history, and geography teacher who has worked with the program for many years. Keba has experience teaching groups and individual lessons, and he has professionally taught five languages: Wolof, French, Mandinka, Pulaar, and Guitare. He has also worked as a linguistics consultant and as an instructor for the Peace Corps Training Center.

Aside from his teaching, Keba is also a professional musician and enjoys exploring facets of the many cultures represented in Senegal. We look forward to the release of his first album!

Scholar Mentor

The community of Yoff in Dakar, Senegal, has welcomed eight years of Princeton Novogratz Bridge Year students to experience Senegalese family, life, and culture. At the heart of this program, Babacar Mbaye has worked tirelessly to support each student in becoming a better global citizen through experiential learning. Communities are facing added challenges right now due to COVID-19, in part because the typical Novogratz Bridge Year Program will not be running in 2020-2021. As students who have been deeply moved by our experiences on Bridge Year, we feel compelled to take this opportunity to support our amazing instructors and the communities that welcomed us, as well know our responsibility to be more sustainable and equitable in these relationships.
